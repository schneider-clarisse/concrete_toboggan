Competition

About the Competition

CNCCC began 20 years ago as a competition for Canadian universities similar to the original ASCE National Concrete Canoe Competition originating in the 1960’s for American schools. The competition involves building a two to four person canoe to race against other schools, along with presenting a technical report on the design process. The competition has since expanded into a number of other countries including Germany, Japan, South Africa and the Netherlands.

Technical Exhibit and Report

Each team creates an interactive booth and writes a technical report showcasing the important design elements of their concrete canoe. The exhibit focuses on design, the construction process, and project management. A panel of professionals reward teams for research and innovation in different design areas based on the written technical report and an oral presentation.

Race Day

On race day, the competition heads to the water! It is here where all the hard work and dedication of the teams finally comes to the finish line. Each university team competes in five races:

  • Men’s 200m Sprint
  • Women’s 200m Sprint
  • Men’s 600m Endurance
  • Women’s 600m Endurance
  • Co-ed 400m Sprint
Teams compete in race heats until an overall winner is crowned at the end of the day. Race day events are open to the public, and we encourage everyone to come out and support the achievements of university students across Canada!
